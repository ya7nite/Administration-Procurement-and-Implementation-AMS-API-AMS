Where the numbers come from

Every cost benchmark in this model is tagged with how it was sourced. This is the honest count, not a marketing claim.

  • Rent (7 locations): 6 of 7 are sourced from public Dubai commercial real-estate market guides; 1 is an inferred estimate. Medium confidence throughout — useful for planning, not a signed lease quote.
  • Staffing (18 roles): 7 of 18 roles are sourced from UAE salary and recruitment sites; the other 11 are inferred from comparable roles where no direct listing existed. Medium confidence throughout.
  • Fit-out cost (9 business units): 3 of 9 are sourced from general interior fit-out cost guides; 6 are inferred working estimates calibrated against those sources. Medium confidence throughout.
  • Procurement & equipment: benchmark unit costs, lead times, and shipping/customs friction are working assumptions calibrated for planning purposes — not live supplier quotes.

Ask about any cost this model assumes — this runs a real web search right now, it's not pulling from the benchmarks above.

Key risks & assumptions

What this model is confident about, and what still needs real-world validation before final investment terms.

  • Bridge markets are estimated, not locally sourced. Turkey, Geneva, and Palma figures use a flat multiplier applied to Dubai benchmarks — there is no local rent or staffing data for those markets yet, which is why their data confidence score is lower.
  • Recurring-revenue share is a working assumption. The % of income treated as membership/subscription per business unit reflects typical mix for that category, not measured historical data from this venture.
  • Cost benchmarks are public-market estimates, not signed quotes. Rent, staffing, and fit-out figures are calibrated from named market sources but should be confirmed with real contracts before committing capital.
  • Timelines assume an on-schedule build-out. Real-world delays in licensing, hiring, or supply chains would push the breakeven month later than shown here.
  • Procurement includes estimated friction, not supplier quotes. Shipping, customs, and installation costs by location are planning estimates and should be replaced with real supplier quotes ahead of committing spend.
